Evolution of Tobacco Tax Policy (2018-2024)

A timeline of the phased increase in the specific tax rate on a pack of 20 cigarettes.

Pre-2018

Only a 20% ad valorem tax was applied.

June 2018

Specific tax of ₦20 per pack introduced, alongside 20% ad valorem tax.

2020

Specific tax increased to ₦58 per pack. Estimated revenue for the year was ₦55 billion.

2022-2024

Specific tax raised to ₦84 per pack, with ad valorem increasing to 30%.

Key Policy Changes for Alcoholic Beverages

The government has phased in specific tax increases to improve revenue generation and public health outcomes.

Pre-2018

Excise duties were based on a 20% ad valorem rate.

2018-2021

Phased-in specific tax increases began, with rates varying by product type (e.g., beer, wine, spirits).

2022

The specific tax on beer was raised to ₦40 per litre.

2024

The specific tax on beer was raised to ₦100 per litre, continuing the trend of increasing specific duties.

Conclusion & Outlook

Key Insights

  • Nigeria's excise tax policies have seen a shift from ad valorem to a mixed tax system with specific duties to better capture revenue and manage public health.
  • Specific revenue data is challenging to find, but reports point to an estimated ₦55 billion from tobacco in 2020 and an estimated ₦20 billion from SSBs in 2022.
  • The alcohol tax policy, while lacking specific revenue data, shows a clear trend of increasing specific tax rates, and a 2016 World Bank report estimated a potential excise revenue of ₦312 billion.
  • Across all categories, there is still significant potential to increase revenue and improve health outcomes by aligning tax rates with international benchmarks.

Future Potential

To optimize the impact of excise taxes, future policy reforms should consider:

  • Adopting inflation-adjusted rates for specific duties.
  • Expanding the tax base to include new products like e-cigarettes.
  • Investing the generated revenue into dedicated public health programs.
